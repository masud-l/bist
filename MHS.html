I am a biomedical researcher specializing in cancer immunology, with a particular focus on understanding how tumors resist radiotherapy and chemotherapy. My doctoral work at Inje University College of Medicine uncovered novel roles for fibroblast growth factor signaling as a biomarker for radiation-induced cross-resistance in colorectal cancer.

Currently, I hold a dual position as a Post-doctoral Researcher at Kyungpook National University and a Scientific Writer at the Joanna Briggs Institute, University of Adelaide. My work bridges laboratory discovery and evidence synthesis, driven by a commitment to translating mechanistic insights into actionable clinical knowledge.

My research interests span protein engineering, tumor immunology, apoptosis pathways, and computational biology. I am equally passionate about scientific communication and bringing rigorous evidence to the forefront of clinical decision-making.
