I am a neuroscientist specializing in the molecular biology of neurodegeneration and aging, with a focus on Parkinson's disease. My doctoral research at Inje University's Biological Clock and Aging Laboratory centered on developing an early diagnostic platform and smart drug delivery system based on alpha-synuclein-enriched exosomal biomarkers.

As a recipient of the Global Korea Scholarship — the South Korean government's most prestigious award for international graduate students — I have spent years at the intersection of wet-lab discovery and evidence-based medicine. My work spans in vitro and in vivo experimentation, systematic reviews, and meta-analyses, reflecting a commitment to translational science.

Currently, I contribute both as a researcher and as a Scientific Writer at the Joanna Briggs Institute, University of Adelaide, where I synthesize evidence to support clinical practice guidelines. I am passionate about bridging laboratory findings and clinical knowledge to ultimately improve outcomes for patients with neurodegenerative disorders.
